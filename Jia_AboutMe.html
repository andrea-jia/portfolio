About Me:

Andrea Jia

Hello! I'm Andrea Jia, and I am currently a freshman at Dublin High School. I am interested in the field of biology, and I am considering a biomedical career in the future. To reach my goals, I am planning on participating in the Physics, Chemistry, and Biology Olympiad, which will allow me to test my knowledge of the subjects I am interested in. I am also taking CSP to broaden my knowledge of technology, as every career in this generation usually involves an aspect of technology, and it will be helpful for me to understand how to code to contribute to any future job. Additionally, I will be applying for the COSMOS program provided by the California State University, which will allow for further enrichment in the field of biology. To me, the study of life and the microscopic components that make that possible are extremely interesting, and has space for contribution. There are many possibilities in the scientific fields, and biology happens to attract my interest the most.

I am a person who values organization, and I tend to keep my workspace as neat as it can be. One of my hobbies is art, so I am also an artistic person. This helps in organization as well as other aspects in future careers. Through my experiences, I've learned that I am also diligent in my work, and that I make sure everything I do is the best it can be. When working with others, I am cooperative in that I listen to what they have to say, and I am open to the the ideas that they have to share.

In middle school, I held Principal's Honor Roll for three years, and participated in Speech and Debate. In high school, I joined numerous clubs, including both academic and volunteer clubs. For example, I have joined the Biology Olympiad Club in order to further develop my interest in biology and to better prepare for the test. I also participated in activities like can food collection and medical supply sorting for community service.

Throughout the course of high school, I hope to extend my skills by experiencing different things and to get a more specific idea of what aspect of biology I am most interested in.
